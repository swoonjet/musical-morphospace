Premise

Every musical tradition on Earth occupies a specific point in a vast abstract space — a mathematical space defined by choices about pitch, rhythm, form, texture, function. Gregorian plainchant is one point. Balinese gamelan is another. Delta blues is another. Each point is a self-consistent grammar.

The world's traditions cluster in dense regions of this space — pentatonic scales, duple meter, verse-refrain form. But vast regions remain empty. They contain musical grammars that are internally coherent, humanly performable, and that no culture happened to develop.

This project builds a curiosity-driven agent that explores those empty regions and documents what it finds.

Each expedition locates a point in the space that sits far from every known tradition, checks that the parameters at that point are internally consistent, and produces two artifacts: a field-note document describing the invented grammar in an ethnomusicologist's voice, and a rendered audio specimen demonstrating it in practice.

The grammar is the artwork. The audio is a specimen.

Featured specimen

N° 007 · Fractal Fugue in Silver Mean Curiosity 0.51 Nearest: Spectral (French school) · Xenakis · Bach fugue

A fugue in a scale that has no octave

Nine equal steps dividing the silver ratio (1+√2 ≈ 2.414) as a non-octave pseudo-octave. The fugal subject rises, answers a "silver-fifth" up, enters in stretto, returns in augmentation at the upper home — then resolves. Home is a function, not a pitch.

Architecture

01sample coordinate in morphospace→30-dim point (18 continuous + 12 categorical)
02coherence check→10 hard constraints + 3 soft warnings
03curiosity score→weighted distance from 84-tradition corpus
04prompt an LLM as field ethnomusicologist→field notes + JSON audio spec
05render audio spec→WAV (additive synthesis, spatial voices)
06archive specimen→markdown + json + wav, one folder per expedition

Corpus

The curiosity metric needs a reference — a set of occupied points to measure distance from. The corpus encodes 84 real-world musical traditions as coordinates in the same 30-dimension space. From Aka Pygmy polyphony to Xenakis stochastic composition. The agent's expeditions must sit far from all of them.

Explore

01
The map
2D projection of the 84-tradition corpus via classical MDS. See which regions are dense and which are empty. Expeditions will appear here as glowing points as the agent runs.
02
Corpus browser
Heatmap matrix of all 84 traditions × 18 continuous dimensions. Sortable, filterable. Click any tradition for its full morphospace coordinates and rationale notes.
03
Featured expedition
Fractal Fugue in Silver Mean — fugal counterpoint in a non-octave scale where the pseudo-octave is a second home rather than an equivalent. Full field notes, 80-second audio, and the scripted audio spec.
04
The 30-dimension schema
Raw JSON. 18 continuous dimensions with calibration examples. 12 categorical dimensions with enumerated values. 10 hard coherence constraints. 3 soft warnings.
05
The system prompt
The ethnomusicologist voice. Output structure. Anti-fiction discipline. Everything the LLM is told about how to document a sampled coordinate — no invented cultures, no romanticism.
06
Run your own expedition
Clone the repo. python3 -m loop.expedition runs the full loop — rejection-sampled coordinate, coherence check, LLM call (Claude API, Ollama, or offline), audio render, archive. All open-source.
